Prototype F2 — Nordic Light

Concept: Maximum restraint, closest to Anthropic's aesthetic. Left-aligned hero with enormous serif type. Content organized in a magazine-style two-column grid (label | content). The animation is barely there — a slow, almost imperceptible drift of pale blue light behind the hero. Every element earns its place. Nothing decorative.

Mood: Authoritative, calm, confident. This is the design equivalent of a quiet room with one perfect painting. It trusts the typography and words to carry the brand, not visual effects. The restraint itself communicates intelligence.

DM Serif Display + DM Sans (from Maple) Near-white / Ice blue / Deep navy accents Barely-there ambient light drift

Why this works: Anthropic's site succeeds because it doesn't try hard. This prototype follows that principle — the large italic serif says everything. The two-column layout (label left, content right) gives the page a publication quality. Focus areas use a clean numbered grid instead of cards. Research is a simple list. Everything breathes.

Our Mission

Making AI approachable for every Canadian

Canada has world-class AI talent, strong democratic values, and a multilingual, multicultural population. We believe these strengths should shape how AI develops — not just in labs, but in communities, classrooms, and homes across the country.

canLM is an applied AI research organization focused on producing public research, tools, and content that help Canadians understand and benefit from advances in artificial intelligence and large language models.
